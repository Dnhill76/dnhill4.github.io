Foundation First

Nutrition

Before any supplement hits your protocol, nutrition is the infrastructure everything else runs on. Get this right and everything upstream gets easier.

The Three Macronutrients

Think of macros like the three systems powering a submarine — propulsion, navigation, and life support. Cut one and the mission degrades.

P

Protein

The primary building block for muscle repair and growth. For high-output individuals, target 0.7–1.0g per pound of bodyweight daily. Prioritize whole food sources: eggs, lean beef, poultry, fish, Greek yogurt.

  • Supports muscle protein synthesis
  • Increases satiety between meals
  • Maintains lean mass during caloric deficits
C

Carbohydrates

The body's preferred fuel source for high-intensity work. Carbs aren't the enemy — mistiming and overconsumption are. Aim for complex sources the majority of the time: oats, rice, sweet potatoes, legumes.

  • Primary fuel for anaerobic work
  • Replenishes muscle glycogen post-training
  • Supports cognitive performance under stress
F

Dietary Fats

Essential for hormone production, joint health, and fat-soluble vitamin absorption. Do not go below 0.3g per pound of bodyweight. Focus on unsaturated sources: olive oil, avocado, nuts, fatty fish.

  • Supports testosterone and hormonal health
  • Reduces systemic inflammation
  • Aids absorption of vitamins A, D, E, K

Meal Timing & Frequency

Meal timing matters most around training. Think of your pre- and post-workout windows like loading and unloading cargo — get the timing wrong and the efficiency drops.

Pre-Workout (1–2 hrs before)

Moderate protein + complex carbs. Avoid high-fat meals immediately before training — fat slows gastric emptying and can blunt energy availability.

Post-Workout (within 60 min)

Prioritize fast-digesting protein (whey or lean meat) with carbohydrates to spike insulin and drive nutrient uptake into muscle tissue. This is when the body is most receptive.

Total Meal Frequency

Research supports 3–5 meals per day as optimal for muscle protein synthesis. Spreading protein across meals — rather than loading it all at dinner — maximizes anabolic response throughout the day.

Wake
Break the fast. Protein + slow carbs.
Pre-WO
Complex carbs + moderate protein.
Post-WO
Fast protein + carbs. Priority window.
Evening
Protein + fats. Limit high-GI carbs.

Setting Your Calorie Target

Calories are the budget. Macros are where you spend it. You can't build or cut effectively without knowing both numbers.

BUILD

Lean Muscle Gain

Caloric surplus of 200–300 calories above TDEE. Minimize fat gain by avoiding aggressive bulks. Expect 0.5–1 lb of total weight gain per week.

CUT

Fat Loss

Caloric deficit of 300–500 calories below TDEE. Maintain high protein to preserve lean mass. Do not crash diet — it accelerates muscle loss.

MAINTAIN

Performance & Maintenance

Match intake to TDEE. Adjust weekly based on weight trend and performance metrics. The goal here is sustained output, not aesthetic change.
