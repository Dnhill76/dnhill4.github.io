Signal vs. Noise

Supplements

The supplement industry is built on hype. This page is built on research. Here's what actually has evidence behind it — and what doesn't make the cut.

The Tier System

Think of supplements like security clearances — not everything that applies gets approved. Only what has the research behind it earns a place in the protocol.

TIER 1

Strong, consistent evidence. Worth including for most people.

TIER 2

Moderate evidence. Situationally useful. Individual response varies.

TIER 3

Weak or mixed evidence. Generally not worth the cost.

TIER 1 High Evidence Supplements

Creatine Monohydrate

Strength & Power

The most researched supplement in sports science. Creatine increases phosphocreatine stores, allowing for greater ATP regeneration during short, high-intensity efforts — think max lifts, sprints, and explosive movements.

Dose3–5g daily. No loading required.
TimingAnytime. Consistency matters more than timing.
FormMonohydrate only. Avoid expensive variants — same effect.

Whey Protein

Muscle Recovery

A fast-digesting, complete protein source derived from dairy. Useful when whole-food protein intake is insufficient or when a post-workout protein source is needed quickly. Not magic — just convenient protein.

Dose20–40g per serving. Match to total protein goals.
TimingPost-workout or between meals as needed.
FormWhey concentrate or isolate. Isolate for lactose sensitivity.

Caffeine

Performance & Focus

Well-documented ergogenic aid. Caffeine blocks adenosine receptors, reducing perceived fatigue and increasing alertness. Effective for both endurance and strength performance when dosed correctly and cycled appropriately.

Dose3–6mg per kg of bodyweight. Start low.
Timing30–60 min pre-training. Avoid within 6 hrs of sleep.
NoteTolerance builds fast. Cycle off periodically.

Vitamin D3

Health Foundation

A critical hormone precursor, not just a vitamin. Deficiency is extremely common in indoor-heavy occupations and northern climates. Affects testosterone production, immune function, mood, and bone density.

Dose2,000–5,000 IU daily. Get bloodwork to confirm level.
TimingWith a fat-containing meal for absorption.
StackPair with Vitamin K2 (MK-7) for optimal calcium regulation.

TIER 2 Moderate Evidence Supplements

Beta-Alanine

Muscular Endurance

Increases carnosine in muscle tissue, buffering acid buildup during sustained high-intensity efforts (60–240 seconds). Most useful for rucking, circuits, and metcons — less relevant for pure strength work. Causes harmless tingling (paresthesia) at higher doses.

Dose3.2–6.4g daily, split into smaller doses.

Omega-3 (Fish Oil)

Recovery & Inflammation

EPA and DHA have documented anti-inflammatory effects, supporting joint health and cardiovascular function. Most useful for those with low dietary fish intake. Modest effect on muscle protein synthesis has also been reported.

Dose2–3g combined EPA+DHA daily.

Magnesium Glycinate

Sleep & Recovery

Deficiency is common in high-stress, high-sweat populations. Magnesium plays a role in muscle relaxation, sleep quality, and over 300 enzymatic reactions. Glycinate form is best absorbed and least likely to cause digestive issues.

Dose200–400mg elemental magnesium before bed.

Ashwagandha (KSM-66)

Stress & Hormonal

Adaptogenic herb with the most clinical data behind it in its KSM-66 extract form. Shown to reduce cortisol, improve stress response, and modestly support testosterone levels in stressed or sleep-deprived individuals.

Dose300–600mg KSM-66 extract daily.

What to Skip

If it's being marketed aggressively, there's a good chance it doesn't need to be — because the research would speak for itself.

BCAAs — Redundant if protein intake is adequate. Whole protein sources contain all essential amino acids already.
Testosterone Boosters — No over-the-counter product meaningfully raises testosterone. Save your money.
Fat Burners — Typically caffeine plus unproven proprietary blends. The caffeine does the work; the rest is filler.
Glutamine — Useful clinically for gut repair but unnecessary for healthy, well-nourished athletes.
